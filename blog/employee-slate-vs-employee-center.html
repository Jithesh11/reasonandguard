EmployeeWorks and Employee Slate, explained — and why not everyone should switch yet

ServiceNow just shipped the biggest change to its employee experience since Service Portal launched in 2016. Here's what's actually new, what's marketing, and how to tell if your org should move.

11 Jul 2026 · 4 min read

Two new names are showing up in every ServiceNow deck this year: EmployeeWorks and Employee Slate. They get used almost interchangeably, which is exactly why they're confusing. Here's the difference, in pictures, before you put either on a roadmap.

The equation

Now Assist and EmployeeWorks aren't competitors — they're the same intelligence layer facing two different audiences. Now Assist helps the people working inside ServiceNow. EmployeeWorks is the front door for the people asking ServiceNow for help in the first place.

Employee Slate isn't a reskin — it runs on AIUX, a different framework entirely.

What migrates on its own

Worth knowing before you plan around this

ServiceNow is not deprecating Service Portal or Employee Center. Both remain fully supported. Employee Slate is early-stage — topic taxonomy, manager hubs, and complex custom page building aren't fully built out yet.

So which one fits you

Content-heavy intranet, deep navigation, lots of legacy widgets? Stay on Employee Center — the rebuild cost outweighs the gain today. A reasonably clean catalog and knowledge base, and ready to go conversational-first? Employee Slate is worth piloting — with a close eye on licensing and token costs, which scale differently than a traditional portal. Either way, it's an instance-specific answer, not a platform-wide one.
